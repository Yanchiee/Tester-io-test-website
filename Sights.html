La Sagrada
Familia

001
Architect
Designed by Antoni Gaudi, who dedicated 43 years of his life to the project, the last 15 exclusively.
002
Construction
Began in 1882 and still under construction — one of the longest-running architectural projects in history.
003
Height
The tallest tower (Jesus Christ) will reach 172.5 meters, making it the tallest religious building in Europe.
004
UNESCO Heritage
Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984, recognizing its outstanding universal architectural value.
Phase 01 — Blueprint
The Iron Lady
Gustave Eiffel's radical vision — a 300-metre iron lattice tower, designed as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair.
Phase 02 — Framework
Engineering Marvel
18,038 pieces of wrought iron joined by 2.5 million rivets. The tower took just 2 years, 2 months, and 5 days to build.
Phase 03 — Assembly
Rising Above Paris
Three observation levels connected by elevators and stairs. At completion, it was the tallest man-made structure in the world.
Phase 04 — Icon
Symbol of France
From controversial structure to global icon — the most-visited paid monument in the world, welcoming nearly 7 million visitors annually.

Eiffel
Tower

001
Engineer
Designed by Gustave Eiffel's engineering company. The structural calculations were done by Maurice Koechlin and Emile Nouguier.
002
Construction
Built from 1887 to 1889 for the Exposition Universelle — completed in a record 26 months with 300 workers on site.
003
Height
Standing at 330 metres including antennas, it remained the world's tallest structure for 41 years until the Chrysler Building.
004
Weight
The iron structure weighs 7,300 tonnes. It is repainted every 7 years, requiring 60 tonnes of paint across three graduated shades.
